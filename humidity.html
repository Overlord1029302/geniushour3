Humidity

What It Is: The amount of moisture in the air.
Why It Matters: Many plants love humidity and can suffer in dry air.
How to Increase It:
  • Misting: Lightly spray leaves with water.
    •
  • Pebble Trays: Set a pot on a tray of pebbles and water to create a humid zone.

Pros:

  • Improved Water Retention: High humidity levels can reduce the rate of water loss through transpiration, helping plants maintain hydration.
  • Enhanced Growth: Many plants, especially tropical varieties, thrive in high humidity conditions, leading to healthier growth and development.
  • Reduced Stress: Adequate humidity can lower stress levels in plants, reducing the risk of wilting and leaf drop.

    • Cons:

  • Disease Growth:Excess humidity can promote the growth of mold, mildew, and other fungal diseases, which can harm plants.

  • Pest Attraction: High humidity levels can attract pests like aphids and spider mites, which thrive in moist environments.
