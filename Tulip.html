Tulip

Scientific Name: Tulipa

Family: Liliaceae

Native Range: Native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

Description: Recognizable for their cup-shaped flowers, tulips bloom in a variety of vibrant colors, making them a favorite in spring gardens.

Care Tips: Prefer well-drained soil and full sun. Plant bulbs in the fall for spring blooming.

Interesting Fact: Tulips were so valuable in the Netherlands during the 17th century that they triggered "Tulip Mania," a period of speculation that led to financial ruin for many investors.

Tulips flourish in climates with cold winters and cool springs, needing well-drained, fertile soil. Planting should be done in the fall, around October to November, in areas with full to partial sunlight. Water the tulips thoroughly after planting and maintain moist, but not waterlogged soil.

Fertilize the bulbs when planting and again in early spring to support growth. After blooming, remove spent flowers to prevent energy loss on seed production and let the leaves die back naturally, which helps store energy for the next season. Regularly check for pests like aphids and slugs and use eco-friendly pesticides as needed.

Tulips, native to Central Asia, became highly prized in the Netherlands during the 17th-century Tulip Mania. Today, there are over 3,000 varieties, symbolizing perfect love and renewal. These beautiful flowers continue to captivate gardeners and flower enthusiasts worldwide
