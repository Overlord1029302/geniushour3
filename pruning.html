Pruning

What It Is: Cutting away dead or damaged parts of a plant.
Why It's Cool:
  • Boosts Growth: Helps the plant focus on new leaves and flowers.
  • Keeps It Neat: Shapes the plant and improves airflow.
How to Do It: Use clean scissors to snip off dead leaves just above a leaf node.

Pros:

  • Promotes Healthy Growth: Pruning removes dead or diseased parts, allowing plants to focus their energy on healthy growth.
  • Enhances Appearance: Regular pruning helps maintain the desired shape and size, making plants look neat and well-kept.
  • Encourages Flowering and Fruiting: Proper pruning can stimulate more blooms and fruit production by improving light penetration and air circulation.
  • Prevents Overcrowding: Thinning out dense growth reduces competition for nutrients and space, which can lead to healthier plants overall.

    • Cons:

  • Risk of Damage:Improper pruning techniques can harm plants, leading to stunted growth or increased susceptibility to disease.
  • Time-Consuming:Pruning can be labor-intensive and requires regular attention to maintain plant health and appearance.
  • Potential for Over-Pruning: Removing too much foliage can stress the plant and reduce its ability to photosynthesize and thrive.
  • Disease Transmission: Using unclean tools can spread diseases between plants, so it's important to sanitize pruning equipment.
