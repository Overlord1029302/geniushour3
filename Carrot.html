Carrot

Scientific Name: Daucus carota

Family: Apiaceae

Native Range: Europe and Asia.

Description: A crunchy root vegetable with a sweet or tangy taste, often eaten raw or cooked in stews and soups.

Care Tips: Carrots require a cool, moist climate to grow. They need well-drained soil and full sun.

Interesting Fact: Carrots were first cultivated in ancient China over 1,000 years ago.

Carrots thrive in well-draining, loose soil with plenty of organic matter. They prefer cool temperatures, ideally between 60-70°F (15-21°C). Plant carrot seeds directly into the ground about 1/4 inch deep in early spring or late summer for a fall harvest. Ensure they receive full sun for optimal growth, though they can tolerate partial shade.

Water carrots consistently, keeping the soil evenly moist to prevent the roots from becoming woody or splitting. Thin the seedlings to allow space for the roots to develop properly. Carrots benefit from regular weeding to reduce competition for nutrients. Apply a balanced fertilizer every 4-6 weeks during the growing season for healthy development.

Originating from Central Asia, carrots are a nutritious root vegetable rich in beta-carotene, fiber, and antioxidants. They come in various colors, including orange, purple, yellow, and white. Carrots are a staple in many culinary dishes worldwide, valued for their sweet flavor and versatility
