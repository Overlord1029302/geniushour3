Potato

Scientific Name: Solanum tuberosum

Family: Solanaceae

Native Range: South America.

Description: A starchy root vegetable with a brown skin and white flesh, often boiled, mashed, or fried.

Care Tips: Potatoes require a cool, moist climate to grow. They need well-drained soil and full sun.

Interesting Fact: Potatoes are a type of nightshade.

Potatoes thrive in well-drained, loose soil rich in organic matter. They prefer cooler temperatures, ideally between 60-70°F (15-21°C). Plant seed potatoes in the early spring, about 2-4 weeks before the last frost date, by cutting them into pieces with at least one "eye" each and planting them 4 inches deep and 12 inches apart in rows spaced 3 feet apart.

Water potatoes consistently, keeping the soil evenly moist but not waterlogged. As the plants grow, mound soil around the stems to protect the developing tubers from sunlight and pests. Fertilize with a balanced, slow-release fertilizer at planting time and again during the growing season. Regularly weed the area to reduce competition for nutrients and space.

Originating from the Andean region of South America, potatoes are a versatile and nutritious staple crop. They come in various types, including russet, red, and fingerling, each with its own growing and culinary characteristics. Rich in carbohydrates, vitamins, and minerals, potatoes are a valuable addition to any garden
