Apples

Scientific Name: Malus domestica

Family: Rosaceae

Native Range: Central Asia.

Description: A sweet or tart fruit with a crunchy texture, often eaten fresh or used in pies and other desserts.

Care Tips: Apples require a cool, moist climate to grow. They need well-drained soil and full sun.

Interesting Fact: Apples come in over 7,500 varieties worldwide.

Apples thrive in well-draining, loamy soil rich in organic matter. They prefer temperate climates with cold winters and mild summers, ideally requiring a period of chilling to produce fruit. Plant apple trees in early spring or late fall, ensuring they receive full sun for at least 6-8 hours a day. Dig a hole wide enough to accommodate the root ball and backfill with soil mixed with compost.

Water apple trees deeply and regularly, especially during dry periods, but avoid waterlogging the soil. Mulching around the base helps retain moisture and suppress weeds. Fertilize with a balanced, slow-release fertilizer in early spring and again in midsummer to promote healthy growth and fruit production. Prune apple trees annually to maintain their shape, remove dead or diseased branches, and improve air circulation.

Originating from Central Asia, apples are one of the most popular fruits worldwide, valued for their sweet and tart flavors. There are thousands of apple varieties, including Fuji, Granny Smith, and Honeycrisp, each with its own unique characteristics. Rich in vitamins, fiber, and antioxidants, apples are a nutritious and versatile addition to any garden.
