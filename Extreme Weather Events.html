Extreme Weather Events

What It Is: Extreme weather events are sudden, severe weather conditions such as droughts, floods, hurricanes, and heatwaves that significantly affect the environment and plant life.
Why It Matters:
  • Boosts Adaptation: Plants may develop resistance or adapt to survive in extreme conditions.
  • Ecological Balance: Can lead to the regeneration of certain ecosystems by clearing out old vegetation and allowing new growth.
How to Do Mitigate It's Effects:To mitigate the impact, water plants consistently, provide shade during extreme heat, and ensure proper drainage to avoid waterlogging.

Pros:

  • Strengthens Plants: Exposure to wind stimulates plants to grow sturdier stems and deeper roots, enhancing their overall resilience.
  • Improves Air Circulation:Wind helps reduce humidity around plants, lowering the risk of fungal diseases.
  • Facilitates Pollination: Wind can carry pollen from one plant to another, aiding in the pollination process for certain plant species.

    • Cons:

  • Risk of Damage: Strong winds can cause physical damage to plants, such as broken branches or uprooted plants.
  • Increased Water Loss: Wind can increase the rate of transpiration, leading to water stress if plants are not adequately hydrated.
  • Erosion: Wind can cause soil erosion, removing the topsoil that is rich in nutrients and essential for plant growth.
