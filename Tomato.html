Tomato

Scientific Name: Solanum lycopersicum

Family: Solanaceae

Native Range: South America.

Description: A juicy fruit with a red skin and green or red flesh, often eaten fresh or used in sauces and soups.

Care Tips: Tomatoes require a warm climate to grow. They need well-drained soil and full sun.

Interesting Fact: Tomatoes were once believed to be poisonous in Europe due to their membership in the nightshade family.

Tomatoes thrive in well-drained, fertile soil with plenty of organic matter. They prefer warm temperatures, ideally between 70-85°F (21-29°C), and need full sunlight for at least 6-8 hours a day. Start seeds indoors 6-8 weeks before the last frost date, or plant seedlings directly in the garden after the danger of frost has passed. Space the plants about 18-24 inches apart in rows spaced 3-4 feet apart.

Water tomatoes consistently, keeping the soil evenly moist but not waterlogged. Mulching around the plants helps retain moisture and suppress weeds. Fertilize with a balanced, slow-release fertilizer when planting and again during the growing season. Support the plants with stakes, cages, or trellises to keep the fruit off the ground and improve air circulation.

Tomatoes, originally from South America, are incredibly versatile and come in many varieties, including cherry, beefsteak, and heirloom types. Rich in vitamins, antioxidants, and flavor, tomatoes are a staple in many cuisines worldwide. Regularly prune the plants to remove suckers and encourage better fruit production
