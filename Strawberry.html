Strawberry

Scientific Name: Fragaria × ananassa

Family: Rosaceae

Native Range: Native to North America.

Description: A sweet, red fruit with tiny seeds on its outer skin, strawberries are delicious eaten fresh or used in desserts, jams, and beverages.

Care Tips: Strawberries prefer well-drained soil and full sun. They require regular watering and benefit from mulch to retain moisture.

Interesting Fact: Strawberries are the only fruit with seeds on the outside, and they are also considered an “aggregate fruit” because they form from multiple ovaries.

Strawberries prefer well-drained, sandy soil rich in organic matter. They thrive in a location that receives full sun, about 6-8 hours per day. Plant strawberry plants in the early spring after the last frost, spacing them about 12-18 inches apart in rows spaced 3-4 feet apart. It's important to plant them so that the crowns are just above the soil level.

Water strawberries consistently, keeping the soil evenly moist but not waterlogged. Mulching around the plants helps retain moisture, suppress weeds, and keep the fruit clean. Fertilize with a balanced, slow-release fertilizer at the time of planting and again during the growing season. Regularly remove any runners to prevent overcrowding and promote better fruit production.

Originating from Europe and North America, strawberries are beloved for their sweet, juicy flavor and versatility in culinary uses. There are many varieties, including June-bearing, everbearing, and day-neutral types, each with their own growing and fruiting habits. Rich in vitamins, fiber, and antioxidants, strawberries are a nutritious addition to any garden
