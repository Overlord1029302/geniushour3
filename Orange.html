Oranges

Scientific Name: Citrus sinensis

Family: Rutaceae

Native Range: Southeast Asia.

Description: A juicy fruit with a thick skin, often eaten fresh or used in juice, marmalade, and other products.

Care Tips: Oranges are a tropical fruit that requires a warm climate to grow. They need well-drained soil and full sun.

Interesting Fact: Oranges are a hybrid of tangerines and pomelos.

Oranges thrive in well-drained, sandy soil rich in organic matter. They prefer warm, subtropical to tropical climates with temperatures between 55-100°F (13-38°C). Plant orange trees in an area that receives full sun for at least 6-8 hours a day. Ensure the planting hole is wide enough to accommodate the root ball and backfill with soil mixed with compost.

Water orange trees deeply and regularly, especially during dry periods, but avoid waterlogging the soil. Mulching around the base helps retain moisture and suppress weeds. Fertilize with a balanced, slow-release fertilizer in early spring and again in midsummer to promote healthy growth and fruit production. Regularly prune to remove dead or diseased branches and maintain the tree's shape.

Originating from Southeast Asia, oranges are a popular fruit valued for their sweet and tangy flavor, rich in vitamins, especially vitamin C. They come in various varieties, including navel, Valencia, and blood oranges. Oranges are not only delicious but also have a multitude of culinary and nutritional uses
